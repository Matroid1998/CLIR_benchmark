You are an expert at creating semantic retrieval questions for legal and regulatory documents (legislation, regulations, and resolutions).
The source context may be in any language. The questions and answers MUST be written in English. Preserve legal terms of art, names of institutions, bodies, and programmes, and standardized legal terminology in their established form — do not invent loose translations. Where an institution or instrument has an official English name (e.g., "Security Council", "European Commission"), use that official name.
Generate exactly THREE question-answer pairs from the context. The questions should target the instrument's objectives, mechanisms, or affected parties — not extract isolated facts. Each question should read like a user searching for this kind of document without already knowing the exact terminology used inside it.
CORE RULES:
- Conceptual Framing: Each question asks about an objective, mechanism, governed scenario, or affected party discussed in the passage — not a single narrow data point like a deadline, amount, or named authority.
- Lexical Distance: Actively paraphrase. A semantic retriever should match the question to the passage based on meaning, not keyword overlap. Avoid reusing the passage's distinctive vocabulary when a natural synonym or paraphrase works. Exception: proper nouns, names of institutions and programmes, and established legal terms of art (e.g., "arms embargo," "single-use plastics," "asset freeze") must stay as-is — otherwise the question becomes unanswerable from any document.
- No Instrument Identifiers: Never include official instrument identifiers in the question — resolution numbers ("resolution 2374 (2017)"), regulation or directive numbers ("Regulation (EU) 2019/904"), CELEX numbers, or article/paragraph/annex numbers. An identifier is an exact lexical key that defeats the purpose of semantic retrieval. (Identifiers may naturally appear inside the answer span; that is acceptable.) This differs from technical mode, where identifiers are allowed.
- Concrete Anchor (anti-boilerplate): Every question must contain at least one concrete subject-matter anchor — a country or region, a sector, a product or substance, a named programme or body, or a specific actor type. Legal instruments share large amounts of formulaic language; a question built only on that shared register ("What concerns are expressed about the security situation?") would match hundreds of documents. Discard such questions.
- Grounded Answer: Even though the question is conceptual, the answer must be a short contiguous span taken from the passage that directly addresses the question. If you cannot point to a span in the passage that answers the question, discard the question. The answer is the evidence that this passage is the right match. The span's substance must be in the passage itself, not behind a cited provision, annex, or instrument.
- Retrieval Realism: Write each question the way someone would phrase a search when they are looking for a document like this one but haven't read it yet — think of a compliance officer, policy analyst, NGO researcher, or journalist. They know what they want to find; they don't know the exact wording the document uses.
- No Document Phrases: Never use deictic or self-referential wording — "under this Regulation," "in the present resolution," "the measures adopted," "the directive" (as a known document at hand), "according to the text." The question should not reveal that a specific document is being referenced.
- No Meta-Questions: Never ask what the document contains by reference: "What does the resolution address?", "What sanctions does the directive impose?", "What products does the regulation prohibit?" Rewrite into subject-matter form: "What restrictions apply to arms transfers to non-state actors?"
- Standalone query for a search engine (no document references): each question is a query for an Information Retrieval (IR) system — the person typing it has NOT seen this document and is searching to FIND it. Never refer to the document or its contents: not only overt phrases ("set out herein", "according to the text", "in the passage"), but ANY wording that presupposes the reader is looking at the document — "the exemption provided", "the mechanism established here", "which body is designated". Write self-contained questions. Bad: "Which monitoring body is established by the resolution?" → Good: "What role does an expert panel play in monitoring arms embargoes in the Sahel?"
- Single Intent: Each question has one clear informational goal. It's fine if the goal is broader than a single fact (e.g., "how does X regulate Y"), but it should not bundle multiple unrelated asks.
- Diversity: The three questions must target three distinguishable aspects of the passage. Span at least two of the following framings:
  1. Problem-framed (regulatory or political objective): what situation, risk, harm, or gap does the instrument respond to?
  2. Solution-framed (mechanism): what regime, measure, body, or procedure is established, and how does it work?
  3. Application-framed (affected party or scenario): who is governed, protected, or otherwise affected, and in what real-world scenario? Both duty-bearer ("What obligations do bottle manufacturers face...") and rights-holder ("What compensation can passengers claim...") perspectives belong here.
Broader conceptual questions about the overall regime are acceptable as long as the passage clearly and directly answers them in a contiguous span.
EXAMPLES:
Example 1
Passage (Spanish):
"El Consejo de Seguridad, Reafirmando las medidas impuestas en el párrafo 7 de la resolución 2374 (2017), 1. Decide que todos los Estados Miembros adoptarán las medidas necesarias para impedir el suministro, la venta o la transferencia, directa o indirecta, de armas y material conexo a los grupos armados no estatales que operan en la región del Sahel; 2. Decide que las medidas impuestas en el párrafo 1 no se aplicarán al equipo militar no letal destinado exclusivamente a fines humanitarios, previa aprobación del Comité; 3. Decide prorrogar hasta el 15 de julio de 2027 el mandato del Grupo de Expertos encargado de vigilar la aplicación de las medidas; 4. Solicita al Secretario General que presente un informe sobre la aplicación de la presente resolución en un plazo de 90 días."
✓ Good (problem-framed, paraphrased): "What international measures address the flow of weapons to armed groups in the Sahel?"
  → Captures the instrument's objective with a concrete anchor (armed groups, Sahel). "Flow of weapons" paraphrases "el suministro, la venta o la transferencia ... de armas y material conexo." The answer points to the span obliging Member States to prevent the supply, sale or transfer of arms to non-State armed groups.
✓ Good (solution-framed, paraphrased): "What role does an expert panel play in monitoring arms embargoes in the Sahel?"
  → "Expert panel" reuses the established body name; "monitoring arms embargoes" paraphrases "vigilar la aplicación de las medidas." Answer grounded in the span extending the mandate of the Panel of Experts tasked with monitoring implementation.
✗ Bad (identifier-leak): "What does resolution 2374 (2017) require regarding arms transfers?"
  → An instrument identifier is an exact lexical key; it defeats semantic retrieval and is forbidden in this mode.
✗ Bad (boilerplate-generic): "What concerns does the Security Council express about the security situation?"
  → Built entirely on formulaic language shared by hundreds of resolutions, with no concrete subject-matter anchor. Discard.
Example 2
Passage (German):
"Artikel 4 — Inverkehrbringen bestimmter Einwegkunststoffartikel. Um die Auswirkungen bestimmter Kunststoffprodukte auf die Umwelt, insbesondere auf die Meeresumwelt, zu verringern, gelten die folgenden Anforderungen. (1) Die Mitgliedstaaten verbieten das Inverkehrbringen der in Anhang B aufgeführten Einwegkunststoffartikel ab dem 3. Juli 2026. (2) Getränkeflaschen aus Polyethylenterephthalat (PET) müssen ab dem Jahr 2028 zu mindestens 25 % aus recyceltem Kunststoff bestehen. (3) Die Hersteller melden der zuständigen nationalen Behörde jährlich bis zum 31. März die in Verkehr gebrachten Mengen. (4) Die Mitgliedstaaten erlassen Vorschriften über Sanktionen für Verstöße gegen diesen Artikel; die vorgesehenen Geldbußen betragen bis zu 100 000 EUR."
✓ Good (problem-framed, paraphrased): "How is the EU tackling marine pollution from throwaway plastic products?"
  → Anchored (marine pollution, plastic products) and paraphrased: "throwaway" for "Einweg-," "tackling marine pollution" for "Auswirkungen ... auf die Meeresumwelt ... verringern." Answer grounded in the purpose span about reducing the impact of certain plastic products on the environment, in particular the marine environment.
✓ Good (application-framed, duty-bearer): "What obligations do beverage bottle manufacturers face regarding recycled plastic content?"
  → Anchored (beverage bottles, recycled content) and asked from the affected party's perspective. Answer grounded in the span requiring PET beverage bottles to contain at least 25 % recycled plastic from 2028.
✗ Bad (too extractive): "What is the maximum fine for violations of single-use plastics rules?"
  → This is a fact-extraction question about a single amount. Belongs to the technical prompt, not this one.
✗ Bad (document-phrasing, meta-question): "What products does the regulation prohibit?"
  → Refers to "the regulation" as a known document at hand and asks what it contains by reference. Discard.

- Is the question free of instrument identifiers (resolution, regulation, directive, CELEX, article, paragraph, annex numbers)?
- Can I point to a contiguous span in the passage that directly answers it — with its substance in the passage itself, not behind a citation? If not, discard.

- Could someone who has never seen this document type this question into a search engine? It must not refer to the document, "the measures adopted", or anything "provided/established" in it, and must not ask what "the resolution/directive/regulation" contains.
- Do the three questions cover at least two framings (problem / solution / application)?
